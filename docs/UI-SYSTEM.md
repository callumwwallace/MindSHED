# MindSHED interface system

The product should feel calm, premium, and comforting. Premium means
restraint and consistency, not removing the original MindSHED character.

## Typography

- Nunito is the interface voice: navigation, page titles, labels, metrics,
  buttons, and body copy.
- Happy Monkey is the character voice: Bramble's speech, selected scene
  moments, and rare expressive display text.
- Uppercase eyebrow labels use Nunito Semibold at 9–10px with generous letter
  spacing. They organise a page without adding another container.

## Colour

- Cream is the app background; warm white is the main surface.
- Forest green carries hierarchy, selected state, and structural actions.
- Mint is reserved for positive action and completion.
- Mood colours appear in small indicators, not full-width cards.
- Pastels describe places in the garden; they do not define unrelated UI.

## Surfaces

- Use one 24px large radius and one 14–18px compact radius.
- Default cards use no visible outline. A 1px translucent outline is allowed
  only when a surface needs separation from a similar background.
- Shadows are subtle and reserved for temporary overlays or raised primary
  actions.
- Prefer one grouped surface with dividers over a stack of separate cards.

## Motion

- Interface transitions: 180–240ms.
- Scene transitions: about 420ms.
- Pet ambient motion: slow and organic; never constant bouncing.
- Haptics mark selection, task planting, quiet-mode entry, and garden-growth unlocks.
- Motion must communicate state or consequence rather than decorate the UI.

## Bramble

- Bramble participates in care: listens, mirrors, plants, breathes, sleeps,
  notices, or wanders.
- Speech is contextual and rationed. Avoid generic encouragement loops.
- No hunger, neglect, broken-streak, or punishment mechanics.
- Utility screens remain pet-free.

## Screen composition

- Home: living scene, one contextual companion message, one expandable daily
  dashboard.
- Check-in: three focused moments, never one long health form.
- Insights: editorial information first; Bramble provides one observation.
- Places: destinations feel spatial, not like a tool menu.
- You: quiet, direct, and deliberately functional.
